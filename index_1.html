01Overview

A semester build for the real entry-level controls workflow.

This project is a graduate team build in the EE 5340 Advanced PLC lab. The scope was to design, program, simulate, and commission a complete unit-level control system for the Truck Powder Ingredient Unload station — the plant skid that pneumatically conveys dry powder ingredients out of a tanker trailer, through a vacuum receiver and airlock, and into one of four ingredient storage tanks downstream.

The unit is realized on a Rockwell Automation CompactLogix 5069-L306ER processor (cell CmpLogix-11) that hangs off the main plant ABClogix4 controller via a 1756-EN2TR Ethernet/IP bridge in slot 7, and is operated from a dedicated FactoryTalk View ME HMI.

The deliverable exercises the full controls workflow — sequence-diagram authorship, AOI-based device programming, PackML-style state management, HMI screen design, and inter-processor messaging — on a real pneumatic-conveying process that shares destination tanks with a parallel Rail Powder Unload unit, so destination-tank coordination and abnormal-condition handling are first-class concerns rather than afterthoughts.

02System Architecture

Distributed I/O, AOI device layer, PackML supervisor.

The unit is organized around a single CompactLogix 5069-L306ER controller that owns all of its own I/O, drives all local field devices, and communicates upstream to the plant ControlLogix through a 1756-EN2TR bridge. Destination-tank coordination — which of four ingredient storage tanks the unload routes to, and the interlock preventing a simultaneous Rail Powder Unload to the same tank — is handled with MSG reads/writes across that bridge.

Layer 01

Sequence Supervisor

One of five sequences active at a time — Transfer, Hold, FilterClean, Shutdown, E-Shutdown — each authored as an explicit step diagram and implemented as a step-latched ladder routine.

Layer 02

Device Layer

Built entirely from vendor AOIs (motor, valve, slide-gate, flop-gate) exposing a consistent .Cmd / .Status / .Fail interface. Sequences never touch raw I/O bits.

Layer 03

Abnormal Monitor

Runs in parallel with whatever sequence is active. Elevates low-vacuum, high-discharge-pressure, device-fail, and valve-feedback faults into the appropriate Alarm / Hold / Shutdown response.

Hardware Stack

CompactLogix 5069-L306ER + distributed I/O

Rev 37 controller. Slot 1: 5069-IB16F 16-pt 24 VDC high-speed sinking input (Series A, Rev 2.001). Slot 2: 5069-OB16F 16-pt sourcing output (Series B, Rev 3.001). Onboard dual Ethernet (A1/A2) configured in Linear / DLR mode so the unit can be chained into a device-level ring with neighboring cells without a dedicated ring switch.

A 1756-EN2TR two-port Ethernet/IP bridge in slot 7 of the plant ABClogix4 chassis provides the handoff up to the plant ControlLogix — the channel for MSG-based destination-tank handshakes between the Truck Powder and Rail Powder unload unit controllers.

Software Stack

Studio 5000 + FT View ME

Ladder Diagram under Studio 5000 Logix Designer. Vendor AOIs for all field devices. UDTs bundle step register, step timer, operator commands, transition flags, per-step outputs. AFI diagnostic rungs in unfinished branches during development.

04Phase-by-Phase Progression

From control narrative to commissioned unit.

01
Sequence Design

Control narrative → step diagrams

Five sequences authored in a single Excel workbook — one sheet per sequence — each captured as an ordered list of steps with entry conditions, output actions, transition conditions, and exit destinations. Transfer is 19 steps; Hold is 14 rows of mid-run manipulation paths; FilterClean is 22 steps including the 0.5s open / closed / 1s pause purge pulses; Shutdown is 17 steps of ordered stop (blower → airlock → valves); E-Shutdown is 5 parallel-action steps with a 15-second lockout timer.
02
AOI Device Layer & Simulation

Wrap every device, force every limit

Imported vendor AOI libraries for motor, valve, slide-gate, and flop-gate device classes and wired every field device into its appropriate AOI instance. AFI rungs sat in front of any half-implemented device call during development so the ladder could be downloaded, scanned, and simulated without firing physical outputs. Simulation forced Sts_Open / Sts_Closed limit-switch bits and the Sts_Running contact on the blower and airlock, stepping the sequences through in controller tags and confirming each step's outputs matched the spreadsheet.
03
HMI & Device Command Screens

P&ID overview + layered pop-ups

A main Remote Operator screen drawn in the style of a classic P&ID — truck silhouette at left, XV valves feeding into the receiver and bag filter, blower, 100 psi purge-air header at upper right, four destination tanks at far right — with color-animated valve bodies, running-state animation on blower and airlock, and live numeric fields for FI-2448, FQI-2448, PI-2442, PI-2443, PI-2445, PI-2446. Two operator pop-ups (Sequence Starts, Operator Selection) and device-level pop-ups (valve Open/Close, blower Start/Stop) for Local Control.
04
Abnormal Conditions & Plant Integration

Eight conditions, three responses

Eight conditions implemented as a parallel monitor routine, mapping process-variable excursions and device-feedback failures into the correct Alarm / Hold / Shutdown response. The shared-destination-tank interlock was implemented with MSG blocks reading the Rail Powder Unload unit's active-destination register out of the plant ControlLogix through the 1756-EN2TR bridge — so the Transfer sequence's "destination clear" precondition reflects plant-wide state rather than a local assumption.
05The Five Sequences

Auto-chaining state machine.

Transfer
19 Steps · Default Run

Selects source valve + destination tank, verifies tank <80% and no Rail conflict, baselines all valves, zeroes totalizer, brings up airlock then blower. Auto-chains to Shutdown when flow drops below 5 lb/min or tank hits 95%.

Hold
14 Rows · Mid-Run

Captures mid-run manipulation options — lifting a single valve, pausing the blower, resuming — with clean exits back to Transfer, forward to Shutdown, or to E-Shutdown.

FilterClean
22 Steps · Maintenance

Opens receiver vent XV-2444, then pulses purge-inlet XV-2443 open for 0.5s / closed / 1s pause — three cycles — before auto-chaining into Shutdown.

Shutdown
17 Steps · Ordered Stop

Brings the blower down first, then the airlock, then closes the nine valves in a defined sequence so the receiver does not slam shut on vacuum.

E-Shutdown
5 Steps · Emergency

Trips blower and airlock in parallel, closes all nine valves in parallel, holds a 15-second lockout timer before the unit will accept another start.

09Outcome

Boots clean, runs all five, refuses the wrong tank.

The final deliverable is a working Truck Powder Ingredient Unload unit that boots cleanly on its CompactLogix controller, presents a complete flow-path overview and device-command HMI to the operator, executes each of the five sequences end-to-end against simulated field I/O, correctly refuses to start a Transfer to a destination tank that the Rail Powder Unload unit is already filling, and elevates low-vacuum, high-discharge-pressure, and device-failure events into the correct Alarm / Hold / Shutdown responses. The most valuable outcome was the end-to-end experience itself — carrying a control-narrative specification through sequence-diagram authorship, AOI-based ladder implementation, HMI screen design, and plant-level interlock negotiation. The same path an entry-level controls engineer walks on every greenfield skid they deliver.
